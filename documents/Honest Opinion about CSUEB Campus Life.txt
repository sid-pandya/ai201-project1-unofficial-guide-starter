You have to make the most out of your experience. I went from 2016-2022, I commuted from only 15 minutes away but became friends with people in the dorms so I got to spend some time with them there. That's when obviously there was more people on campus, and more people willing to be apart of things or interact with one another. It's such a beautiful campus. I work as Staff now, so parking is more accessible, housing is available, food options varies but there are food trucks that also come weekly. There are a bunch of clubs and orgs to join, I highly suggest if you are social or want to build those skills to. There's tutoring, career support, and faculty who is willing to work with their students. I think this campus does have a lot to offer, you just have to learn where to go and what it is that you want to do. Easy access to the AC transit, good off-campus food options, BART is close by. I think the campus is slowly but surely coming back to life again post-covid.
